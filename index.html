Strategy Overview

The robot is tuned to play a curling-style endgame: establish defensive puck positions early, then shoot multiple pieces near the end.

  • First phase: place defensive pucks to limit opponent scoring lanes.
  • Middle phase: move own pieces into prepared shooting positions.
  • Final phase: rapid multi-shot launch sequence while holding heading.

Scoring target zones:

  • 24 in ring: 1 point
  • 16 in ring: 2 points
  • 8 in ring: 5 points

Goal is to keep pieces in strong positions until the final shots are fired.

Drivetrain and Frame

  • 4-wheel mecanum layout for holonomic movement and fast lateral correction.
  • 200 RPM, 12V DC motors selected as a cost/performance balance.
  • L298N motor drivers with an XL6019 boost stage for robust supply overhead.
  • 3D-printed motor mounts integrated into an aluminum frame.

The frame is optimized for predictable straight-line motion and quick repositioning between load, defensive placement, and shoot zones.

Shooter and Piece Handling

  • Flywheel launcher maintains wheel speed between shots for quick bursts.
  • Reload path keeps game pieces close to the ground for stable feed-in.
  • Shooter wheel and feed mechanism are staged for repeated multi-piece firing.
  • Beam-break sensing confirms piece transitions through the feed path.

The mechanism is designed for both deliberate placement and aggressive final volleys, depending on game state.

Autonomy, Sensors, and State Machine

  • Two ultrasonic sensors estimate X/Y position from known arena walls.
  • IMU used to hold heading and reduce drift during straight-drive segments.
  • Beam-break sensors support puck detection and shooter sequencing.
  • Optical and long-range ultrasonic piece-detection approaches were tested and rejected for reliability limits.

FSM structure separates motion states (drive_home, drive_shoot_pos, idle) from shooter states (shoot_piece, reload, unload, done) for clearer behavior control.

Cycle Timing Plan

Baseline motion estimate: ~4 seconds travel per leg at approximately 0.5 m/s.

Cycle 1 (~18s)
Travel to launch zone, fire ~3 pucks for defense, return.
Cycle 2 (~15s)
Reload, travel, drop ~2 pieces on ground for setup, return.
Cycle 3 (~15s)
Reload, travel, drop ~3 pieces into setup cluster, return.
Endgame
Wait window, then final two shooting cycles to clear staged pucks quickly.
